Visiting Boston?

Are you a student, tourist, or newcomer to Boston? Law and Order: Boston's visualization gallery is here to help you explore all the information you need to keep yourself safe! Browse real data and explore recent crime trends (June 2015 to November 2019).

As Boston residents ourselves, we hope our project will illuminate crime patterns and help keep others out of harm’s way.

Where Do Crimes Occur?

Certain crimes, such as homicide, happen predominantly in only a couple neighborhoods. Others, like burglary, are distributed pretty evenly throughout Boston. The bottom line is that Boston is a safe city, but in any city, it’s necessary to exercise caution.

The map to the right is colored by the number of crimes in each Boston district. SELECT a year and type of crime from the dropdowns to investigate the crimes by district (and to compare total counts versus per capita rates). HOVER over a district to display the number of specified crimes for specified years.

Comparing Districts

Below we show the distribution of total crime across the 12 districts of Boston. Each chart shows the percentages of city-wide crime attributed to the district.

However, the population of Boston is not distributed evenly; there's a huge difference between districts. When you account for this, the graphs paint a different picture. SELECT from the drop-down menu to switch between views to see how the crime rates for each vary from the city-wide average.

Percent of Total Incidents Attributed to Each District

From the initial view, you'd think Dorchester and Roxbury had the highest crime rates. But once we account for population, you see how these numbers change. Dorchester is actually below average for crime across the board, while Roxbury is still above but nowhere near the level we see in Mattapan.

Are You A Night Owl? Snowbird?

We can now see where the crimes are taking place, but when are they occuring? Are some areas safer at night, in the morning, etc.? By interacting with the data you can see how different crimes rise and fall throughout the city during the day.

DRAG your mouse over the timeline next to the drop-down in the bar chart visualization above to see how the numbers change.

A Closer Look

What specific kinds of crime are occurring? Assaults are the most common crime in many districts, followed by vandalism. Robbery and burglary counts tend to reflect how residential or commercial a district is.

SELECT a neighborhood and year from the dropdown to compare quantities of each crime type. The bubbles will shift when one category overtakes another.

CLICK on a bubble for more details about a category. Click again to return to the main view.

Conclusion

Crimes will take place in any major city, so it’s always important to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings. If you are traveling, studying, or looking for a new place to live, understanding crime trends can help keep you safe. We hope our site has helped you feel more knowledgeable as you prepare to embark to Boston!
