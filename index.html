Overview of City Crime Data

In todayâ€™s data-driven world, city governments collect vast amounts of information about every aspect of public life. Crime data, in particular, can help cities take steps to improve the personal and social well-being of their citizens. Knowing where and when most crime happens can allow cities to allocate more resources at necessary places and times to maximize public safety.

In addition, knowing the most common types of crime in a given area can help cities prepare security forces against new and emerging threats. What are the crime trends in this beautiful city we call our home? As Boston residents ourselves, we hope that our project will illuminate crime patterns for all those in the Boston area.

Caught Red-Handed

The map above is colored by the number of crimes in each Boston police district. Use the dropdowns to specify years and types of crime you would like to investigate. Hover over a district to display the number of specified crimes for specified years.

There are certain neighborhoods that Bostonians historically think of as crime-ridden. Are these perceptions rooted in reality, or are they stereotypes? And how much does type of crime affect these perceptions? Certain crimes, such as homicide, happen predominantly in only a couple neighborhoods. Others, like burglary, are distributed pretty evenly throughout Boston.

How accurate are your perceptions of Boston crime?

The City of Boston is broken up 12 police districts. Each has its own set of challenges for police to face. In the above visual we display the 6 major types of crime and break out the reports per district.

From the initial view, you'd think that Dorchester and Roxbury have the highest crime rate. But, this view is only showing you the total percentage of city-wide crime. It does not account for population. Switch the view using the drop-down and see how the values change. Notice Dorchester, which previously looked like a high-crime area, is now below average for every type of crime shown.

We can now see where the crimes are taking place, but when are they occuring?
Are some areas safer at night, in the morning, etc.? By interacting with the data above you can see how different crimes rise and fall throughout the city during the day. Drag your mouse over the timeline next to the drop-down to see how the numbers change.

SELECT a neighborhood and year from the dropdown to compare quantities of each crime type. The bubbles will shift when one category overtakes another.

For many neighborhoods, such as Roxbury, Mattapan, and South End, the total number of crimes skyrocketed between 2015 and 2016.

Roxbury has the greatest number of crimes, especially in more serious crime categories such as Homicides and Rapes.
