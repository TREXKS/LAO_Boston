Visiting Boston?

Are you a student, tourist, or newcomer to Boston? Law and Order: Boston's visualization gallery is here to help you explore all the information you need to keep yourself safe! Browse real data and explore recent crime trends (June 2015 to November 2019). As Boston residents ourselves, we hope our project will illuminate crime patterns and help keep others out of harm’s way.

Where Do Crimes Occur?

Certain crimes, such as homicide, happen predominantly in only a couple neighborhoods. Others, like burglary, are distributed pretty evenly throughout Boston. The bottom line is that Boston is a safe city, but in any city, it’s necessary to exercise caution.

The map to the right is colored by the number of crimes in each Boston district. SELECT a year and type of crime from the dropdowns to investigate the crimes by district (and to compare total counts versus per capita rates). HOVER over a district to display the number of specified crimes for specified years.




Comparing Districts (6/15/15 - 11/15/19) :

Percent of Total Incidents Attributed to Each District

The bar charts below compare districts in terms of percent of citywide incidents and per capita variation from the average number of incidents, both by type of crime. SELECT a measure to update the bars. Use the BRUSH to filter by times of day. HOVER over a bar to view the number of incidents.

The City of Boston is broken up 12 police districts. Each has its own set of challenges for police to face. In the above visual we display the 6 major types of crime and break out the reports per district.

From the initial view, you'd think that Dorchester and Roxbury have the highest crime rate. But, this view is only showing you the total percentage of city-wide crime. It does not account for population. Switch the view using the drop-down and see how the values change. Notice Dorchester, which previously looked like a high-crime area, is now below average for every type of crime shown.

For many neighborhoods, such as Roxbury, Mattapan, and South End, the total number of crimes skyrocketed between 2015 and 2016.

Roxbury has the greatest number of crimes, especially in more serious crime categories such as Homicides and Rapes.

Conclusion

Crimes will take place in any major city, so it’s always important to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings. If you are traveling, studying, or looking for a new place to live, understanding crime trends can help keep you safe; we hope our site has helped you feel more knowledgeable as you prepare to embark to Boston!
